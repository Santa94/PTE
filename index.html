Hi Carla

I hope all’s well with you. It’s been a busy time here and I’m looking forward to
going on holiday next week. I really need a break now, especially after the horrendous
week I’ve had.As you know, over the past few weeks I’ve sent out loads of job
applications.I’ve been wanting
to move on for quite a while now, and have basically
been sending my CV outto any company that looks even vaguely interesting. Some
have been in answer to advertisements and others just on spec as you never know. The
feedback has been quite positive in general (if they bother to answer at all) but I must
tell you about the last interview I had. It was my worst nightmare!

First of all, it was pouring with rain. I’d planned what I was going to wear so I could
tick that off the list but in the end I had to rethink as I’d been intending to wear a
white skirt. Couldn’t risk it! And then there’s always the question of what to do with
your umbrella and everything is damp. Not a disaster really I know, but somehow I
took it as a sign. Luckily there were plenty of buses, so I got to the offices in good
time, and checked in with the receptionist – who was less than friendly. She waved
me over to the waiting area and didn’t even offer me a coffee. By this point I was
starting to feel a littleuncomfortable.You know when your gut instinct is telling you
that something’s not quite right.The general atmosphere wasn’t very welcoming, and
no one seemed to have time to exchange a few words.

By this point I knew that I didn’t want the job so I decided to see the interview as
a useful experience. They asked all the usual questions, you know, about working well
in a team, using initiative, being a people person, keeping calm under pressure etc. Of
course I answered positively and then even asked them a few questions about the
position I was applying for and the structure of the company. And guess what, a
couple of days later I got a letter with the usual ‘we regret to inform you …’ but I can
only say I was relieved, although it would have been good to have been able to turn
them down. Hopefully by the next time we see each other I’ll have a new job. Fingers
crossed!
Take care and write soon.
Love
Sandi
